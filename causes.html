Many factors can cause migraines, and while we have a solid understanding of many of these triggers, some reasons are still unknown. Due to the intricacy of migraines, each person may have different triggers, and at times a number of factors may work together to cause an attack. Some of the most known and thoroughly studied migraine causes are listed below:

  1. Hormonal changes in women: Fluctuations in estrogen, such as before or during menstrual periods, pregnancy, and menopause, seem to trigger headaches in many women.
  2. Drinks: These include alcohol, especially wine, and too much caffeine, such as coffee.
  3. Stress: Stress at work or home can cause migraines.
  4. Sensory stimuli: Bright or flashing lights can induce migraines, as can loud sounds. Strong smells — such as perfume, paint thinner, secondhand smoke and others — trigger migraines in some people.
  5. Sleep changes: Missing sleep or getting too much sleep can trigger migraines in some people.
  6. Sensory stimuli: Bright lights and sun glare can induce migraines, as can loud sounds. Strong smells, such as perfume, and paint thinner can also trigger migraines.
  7. Physical strain: Intense physical exertion, including sexual activity, might provoke migraines.
  8. Weather changes: A change of weather or barometric pressure can prompt a migraine.
  9. Medications: Oral contraceptives and vasodilators, such as nitroglycerin, can aggravate migraines.
  10. Food: Aged cheeses and salty and processed foods might trigger migraines. So might skipping meals.
  11. Food additives: include the sweetener aspartame and the preservative monosodium glutamate (MSG), found in many foods.
